About Me

Hello! My name is Yuntae Ju, and I am a researcher working at the intersection of statistical machine learning, longitudinal data analysis, and human performance modeling. My research focuses on developing data-driven and interpretable modeling frameworks to study complex behavioral and physiological processes in longitudinal and observational settings, with applications to elite sports and population-level health.

I specialize in machine learning–based prediction and risk modeling, mixed-effects and hierarchical modeling, and time-series analysis. A central emphasis of my work is on handling repeated measures, uncertainty, and rigorous model validation, including grouped cross-validation and resampling-based inference. I am particularly interested in integrating flexible ML methods with statistically principled approaches to ensure robustness, interpretability, and transparency in high-stakes evaluation contexts.

Much of my applied research has involved large-scale athlete monitoring data, where I analyze performance trajectories and atypical deviations using ML-based predictive models alongside longitudinal statistical frameworks. While my background includes elite sport analytics, my broader interests lie in population health monitoring, health policy, and fairness-aware statistical modeling. My long-term goal is to develop methodological frameworks that support evidence-based decision-making while carefully distinguishing prediction from causal inference.

Representative Publication

Performance Modeling for Men’s 100m Freestyle: An Artificial Intelligence and Longitudinal Analysis Approach

Ju, Y., Oh, M., Lee, J., Kim, Y., Jang, S. J., Yoo, J., Park, K.-N., Kim, Y., Ryoo, H., Kim, D.-H., & Suh, S.-H.
Scientific Reports, 2026
This study examined elite men’s 100m freestyle performance using an integrated framework combining artificial intelligence (AI), explainable AI (XAI), and longitudinal mixed-effects modeling. Competition records were analyzed with ensemble-based machine learning models trained using athlete-level grouped cross-validation and bootstrap resampling, achieving strong predictive accuracy. SHAP-based interpretation identified athlete age as the most influential predictor, followed by national economic context, while longitudinal mixed-effects modeling revealed a U-shaped age–performance trajectory with peak performance at 23.7 years. Collectively, the framework demonstrates how prediction, interpretability, and longitudinal modeling can be jointly leveraged for robust and transparent performance evaluation in elite sport.

Clustering Rookie Korean Baseball Pitchers and Predicting Potential Using Machine Learning

Ju, Y., Ryoo, H.-J., & Suh, S.-H.
Journal of Coaching Development, 2023
This study evaluates the latent potential of rookie pitchers in the Korea Baseball Organization (KBO) by identifying pitcher archetypes using K-means clustering and examining their association with early-career performance (WAR). Using debut-season physical and performance indicators, we show that high-velocity pitcher types contribute most substantially to team success. A positive correlation between fastball velocity and WAR further suggests that pitching velocity is a key signal for draft evaluation, highlighting the value of machine learning–based analysis for talent identification in professional baseball.
