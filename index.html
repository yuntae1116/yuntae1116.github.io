About Me

Hello! My name is Yuntae Ju, and I am a researcher working at the intersection of machine learning, optimization, and human performance modeling. My work focuses on developing data-driven and model-based approaches to understand complex behavioral and physiological dynamics, particularly in high-stakes environments such as elite sports and population-level health.

I specialize in ML-driven prediction, anomaly detection, mixed-effects modeling, and time-series analysis, with an emphasis on how these methods can support optimal decision-making under uncertainty. Much of my applied research involves large-scale athlete monitoring and anti-doping analytics, where I use computational models to detect performance irregularities, quantify risk, and improve fairness and transparency in evaluation systems.

Beyond sports analytics, I am deeply interested in health policy, resource allocation, and fairness-aware modeling. I aim to build frameworks that connect machine learning with optimization and evidence-based policy design, enabling more equitable and efficient decisions in both elite performance settings and public health contexts.
