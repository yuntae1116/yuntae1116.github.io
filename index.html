About Me

Hello! My name is Yuntae Ju, and I am a sports data scientist and graduate researcher specializing in AI-driven athlete performance analysis, anomaly detection, and large-scale sports data integration. I combine machine learning, exercise physiology, and data-driven modeling to understand and predict complex human performance outcomes.

Beyond elite sports analytics and anti-doping research, I am also deeply interested in health policy, long-term athlete monitoring, and how data-driven models can support fair, transparent, and equitable decision-making in both elite and public health environments.

My research spans across performance prediction, mixed-effects modeling, time-series analysis, wearable-based monitoring, and optimization frameworks for decision-making in sports and health contexts. I aim to bridge artificial intelligence with evidence-based policy to contribute to healthier, safer, and more effective environments for both athletes and the general population.
