About Me

Hello, my name is Yuntae Ju. My research focuses on statistical machine learning, longitudinal data analysis, and interpretable modeling for complex observational data. I am particularly interested in how repeated measurements evolve over time and how meaningful patterns can be identified within heterogeneous populations.

My academic background originates from quantitative analysis in human performance and physiological data, which led me to broader questions about how complex biological and behavioral systems can be modeled using data-driven methods. Through this work, I became increasingly interested in the statistical challenges associated with longitudinal and hierarchical datasets, where observations are collected repeatedly across individuals and where traditional modeling assumptions are often violated.

A central theme of my work is the integration of modern machine learning methods with statistically principled modeling strategies. While machine learning models can offer strong predictive performance, they must be carefully validated and interpreted when applied to real-world scientific data. For this reason, my research emphasizes rigorous evaluation procedures such as grouped cross-validation, resampling-based validation, and model interpretation techniques.

Methodologically, I am interested in problems involving longitudinal prediction, hierarchical modeling, anomaly detection, and interpretable machine learning. Many real-world datasets contain complex dependence structures, including repeated measurements, individual-level heterogeneity, and temporal variation. Developing models that properly account for these structures is essential for producing reliable and interpretable results.

Much of my applied work uses observational datasets related to human performance and population-level measurements as empirical testbeds for methodological development. However, my broader research interest extends to biomedical data science and population-level modeling problems in which robust statistical inference and reliable pattern detection are crucial.

My long-term goal is to develop statistically grounded machine learning frameworks for complex longitudinal data, particularly in settings where prediction, interpretability, and uncertainty quantification must be considered simultaneously.

Representative Publications

Performance Modeling for Men’s 100m Freestyle: An Artificial Intelligence and Longitudinal Analysis Approach

Ju, Y., Oh, M., Lee, J., Kim, Y., Jang, S. J., Yoo, J., Park, K.-N., Kim, Y., Ryoo, H., Kim, D.-H., & Suh, S.-H.
Scientific Reports, 2026
This study analyzes elite men’s 100m freestyle performance using a framework that integrates machine learning and longitudinal modeling. Predictive models were evaluated using grouped cross-validation and resampling-based validation. Model interpretation revealed key predictors of performance and the longitudinal analysis identified a peak performance age around the mid-twenties.

Clustering Rookie Korean Baseball Pitchers and Predicting Potential Using Machine Learning

Ju, Y., Ryoo, H.-J., & Suh, S.-H.
Journal of Coaching Development, 2023
This study examines rookie pitchers in the Korea Baseball Organization using machine learning techniques. K-means clustering was applied to identify pitcher archetypes and evaluate their relationship with early career performance indicators. The results demonstrate how data-driven methods can support performance evaluation and talent identification in professional sports.
